Privacy Policy

Last updated:

We respect your privacy. This policy explains what data Match FEAR (“the Game”) processes and why. If you have questions, contact us at info@example.com.

What we process

  • Account & authentication: When you sign in, the Game uses Epic Online Services (EOS) to authenticate you. We receive an ID token from EOS to validate your session; we do not see your password.
  • Gameplay & leaderboards: Scores, display name (from EOS), and limited telemetry needed to detect cheating and maintain fair leaderboards.
  • Diagnostics (optional): Crash logs or error reports may be collected to fix issues. These logs avoid personally identifiable information where feasible.

How we use data

  • To provide core features: sign‑in, global & friend leaderboards, and cloud entitlements.
  • To prevent abuse and keep the service secure.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

Data sharing

We share data only with service providers needed to run the Game (for example, Epic Online Services for auth and leaderboards). We don’t sell personal data.

Children

The Game is not directed to children under 13. If you’re a parent or guardian and believe a child provided data, contact us to remove it.

Your choices

  • You can sign out at any time from the in‑game menu.
  • To request access, correction, or deletion of data associated with your EOS account ID, contact us.

Data retention

Leaderboard entries and required identifiers are kept while the Game and its services are active. Diagnostics (if any) are kept only as long as necessary to troubleshoot issues.

Third‑party policies

Epic’s privacy policy applies to EOS account data. See Epic Games Privacy Policy.

Changes

We may update this policy as the Game evolves. We’ll update the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, provide in‑game notice.
